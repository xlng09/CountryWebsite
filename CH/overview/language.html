Official Language & Dialects

China’s official language is Mandarin (Modern Standard Chinese), based on the northern dialect with Beijing pronunciation as the standard. China boasts a wide variety of dialects, mainly categorized into 10 major dialect groups: Mandarin, Jin, Wu, Min, Hakka, Yue (Cantonese), Xiang, Gan, Hui, and Pinghua-Tuhua. Geographically widespread, representative dialects include Wu (e.g., Shanghai dialect), Yue (Cantonese), and Min.
