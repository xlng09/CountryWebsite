Landforms Overview

China’s terrain is characterized by highlands in the west and lowlands in the east, forming a stepped distribution. It encompasses diverse landforms: mountains, plateaus, hills, basins, plains, and deserts. Mountains and plateaus are concentrated in the west, while plains and hills dominate the east, divided into three major topographic steps descending from west to east.
