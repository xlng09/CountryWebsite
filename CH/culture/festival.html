Chinese Festivals & Customs

Spring Festival

From New Year’s Eve to the 15th day of the first lunar month. Customs: pasting couplets and New Year paintings, reunion dinners, staying up late, red envelopes, dragon/lion dances, firecrackers.

Learn More

Lantern Festival

15th day of first lunar month. Customs: admiring lanterns, solving lantern riddles, eating glutinous rice balls (yuanxiao/tangyuan).

Learn More

Qingming Festival

April 4–6. Customs: tomb-sweeping, ancestor worship, spring outings, planting willows.

Learn More

Dragon Boat Festival

5th day of the fifth lunar month. Customs: eating zongzi, dragon boat races, hanging wormwood and calamus, drinking realgar wine.

Learn More

Mid-Autumn Festival

15th day of the eighth lunar month. Customs: family reunion, eating mooncakes, admiring the full moon.

Learn More

Double Ninth Festival

9th day of the ninth lunar month. Customs: appreciating chrysanthemums, climbing mountains, drinking chrysanthemum wine.

Learn More

Winter Solstice

Eating dumplings (northern China) or glutinous rice balls (southern China).

Learn More

Laba Festival

8th day of the twelfth lunar month. Customs: drinking Laba porridge.
