History of China

History Overview

China’s long-standing civilization spans thousands of years, evolving through dynasties and shaping a unique cultural foundation.

Prehistoric Civilization & Shang-Zhou Period

The Shang and Zhou dynasties marked the height of China’s Bronze Age with advanced bronze craftsmanship, oracle bone script, and a ritual-centered society.

Unified Empires: Qin & Han Dynasties

The Qin-Han era unified China and built a centralized political system. Agriculture, crafts, and technology advanced, and Confucianism became dominant in the Han.

Three Kingdoms, Jin & Northern–Southern Dynasties

A turbulent era marked by regime changes, social restructuring, and ethnic integration, deeply influencing literature, philosophy, and culture.

Tang & Song: Prosperity & Technology

The Tang saw cultural openness and economic strength; the Song reached global peaks in economy and technology such as printing, gunpowder, and the compass.

Modern History & Founding of the Republic

From the Opium War to the May Fourth Movement, China fought imperialism and feudalism. The founding of the PRC in 1949 marked a historic victory.

Contemporary Development

China has achieved world-class progress in economy, technology, infrastructure, and social systems, becoming a global leader in many fields.
